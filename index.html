Culture

We all live in competitive environments with no easy road to success. The difference usually comes down to a winning culture. It's the key to identifying, addressing and overcoming the inevitable challenges along the way.

Brand building is at the heart of this. The ability to create a shared sense of mission where everyone is inspired to put their individual talents towards the collective good. It might sound a bit soft, but time and time again, it's been the foundation of great work with outsized results.

I'm currently in Bangkok where we've been growing our startup across Southeast Asia. Next we'll be returning home to Sausalito, CA where our kids, community and floating home all reside.

Career Experiences

Brand value represents about 25% of enterprise value. Sometimes a little less, sometimes a lot more. Yet, too few companies realize their full brand potential.

From my experiences with great brands, companies and colleagues, I've learned the art, science and human factors that drive marketing impact — how to keep strategy aligned with business, product and sales realities, and how culture can be the ultimate competitive advantage.

Brands

Every brand challenge is unique: from startups to disruptors to market leaders. But there are timeless truths that underpin all success stories. The often overlooked magic of brand building is the value of aligning internal teams to achieve external success. Even great strategies fail to deliver unless they're embraced across the organization.

Work

Cases & Capabilities

Microsoft
From Anti-trust Target to Customer-Centric Brand

Microsoft was viewed as a monopoly in the early 2000's, with few friends in the tech industry or government. As global strategy lead across creative and media, my role was to create a campaign approach that would shift their brand perceptions to create a more favorable regulatory and consumer environment. The resulting "Your Potential is Our Passion" campaign quickly went from regional to U.S. to 22 global markets — a significant revenue catalyst for McCann-Worldgroup.

adidas
Bringing a Famous Brand Back from Hard Times

There was a time when adidas was in danger of having to leave the U.S. market. Our approach was to redefine the category from Nike and Nike imitators to Nike and adidas. Our "Long Live Sport" campaign tapped into the variety of ways every athlete celebrates participation and achievement. Within one year, adidas had risen to clear #2 — a new, lucrative status in consumer and sport culture.

essentia · GiftNow · Blue Nile · Virgin America
Helping Growth Stage Companies Gain Brand Value

Early stage companies present unique brand challenges. They're up against established competitors who have claimed profitable market positions. Success often relies on approaching these challenges as category creation instead of just brand positioning. Virgin America changed the air travel category to hospitality & entertainment. Blue Nile reimagined jewelry as an experience for the male buyer.
